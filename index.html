Learning How to Learn

Overview  |  Topics  |  Learning Techniques

Overview

Learning How to Learn is an online course that is free-to-audit. The course is taught by national treasure, Barbara Oakley, Ph.D..

The course title is an accurate description of the subject matter. Dr. Oakley teamed up with computational neuroscientist, Terrance Sejnowski, Ph.D., to develop a lesson plan that's based in behavioral and neuroscientific research, but is presented in such an approachable way that it feels like regular common sense.

Learning How to Learn is the first MOOC that I ever completed in its entirety. Taking this class cultivated an enthusiasm for learning in me t hat I have found to be very beneficial for my personal and professional well-being. After completing the course, I truly believe that given enough time and access to the internet, the average person can learn how to do almost anything.

Topics

Focused Learning

When you are actively studying a subject, you are practicing focused learning. Examples of focused learning include reading a book, watching a lecture, or practicing a scale on an instrument. Basically, focused learning is when you are consciously doing work to familiarize yourself with a new concept.

Diffuse Learning

Diffuse learning occurs in times when you are not actively engaged in learning anything at all. It's the reason why people have great ideas in the shower and it's the reason why after 6 years of cramming for French tests in highschool, I can barely remember how to count to neuve (and just had to google the spelling).

The concept of diffuse learning was completely unknown to me before taking the course, but I think it makes a lot of sense. In order for an idea to really stick, your brain is going to need a minute to rest and reconfigure.
